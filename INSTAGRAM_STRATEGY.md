# Appa & Amma's Pickles — 90-Day Instagram Growth Strategy

**North-star goal:** First **100 paying customers** in 90 days, fully organic, ₹0 ad spend.
**Operating reality:** 1 person on content (you / a daughter / cousin), shooting on a phone, 30–45 min/day.
**Winning principle:** People don't follow pickle brands. They follow **a family**. Every post should make a stranger feel like they know Amma a little better than yesterday.

## 1. Profile Setup

### Account type
- **Instagram Business account** (not Creator) — needed for shop tagging, WhatsApp button, and Insights.
- Linked to a Facebook Page (mandatory for shop + ad retargeting later).

### Username
- Primary: `@appaammaspickles`
- Backup if taken: `@appaamma.pickles` or `@appaammakitchen`

### Display name (the searchable field — most brands waste this)
> **Appa & Amma's Pickles | Homemade in our village**

This packs three search keywords (pickles, homemade, village) that show up in Instagram search beyond your handle.

### Profile photo
- **Not the logo.** Use a tight crop of Amma's hands holding a jar, or Amma + Appa smiling, slightly out of focus, warm light.
- Logo lives on the label, not the profile pic. People follow people.

"Food & Beverage Company" → switch to **"Cooking School"** or **"Local Business"** if you want softer placement. Test both for 2 weeks each.

### Action buttons
- **WhatsApp** (primary CTA — this is where sales happen)
- **Email**
- **Order Food / Shop** linked to Dukaan/Shopify store

### Link in bio
Use a **single link** to a Linktree-style page (Beacons, Linktree, or a custom `/links` page on your site) with:
1. 🛒 Order on WhatsApp (top — biggest tap target)
2. 🥭 Shop our pickles
3. 📖 Read our story
4. 🎁 Send a jar home (gifting)
5. 💌 Join Amma's WhatsApp list (for batch alerts)

## 4. Reel Ideas (the growth engine — 90% of new followers will come from here)

Reels are the only format Instagram is actively pushing for new accounts. Plan to ship **4 Reels/week**.

### The 6 Reel formats that work for homemade food brands

#### Format A: "Hands & process" (highest watch-time)
No face, no music distraction — just hands doing one mesmerising step.
- **Reel 1:** Cutting 5 kg of raw mango on the floor (10s of pure chopping, ASMR)
- **Reel 2:** Tempering mustard seeds in a kadai — close-up of the *splutter*
- **Reel 3:** Pouring gingelly oil into a jar of red masala (slow-mo)
- **Reel 4:** Sun-drying lemon halves on a cotton sheet — time-lapse over 6 hours
- **Reel 5:** Hand-pounding red chilli in a stone mortar
- **Reel 6:** Jar lids being hand-wrapped with red gingham cloth + jute twine

Caption template: *"Step 4 of 11. This is how Amma's [pickle] gets its [property]."*

#### Format B: "The story behind one ingredient"
- **Reel 7:** "This mango tree is older than my father." (Pan up the tree, end on Appa.)
- **Reel 8:** "Why we still use stone, not steel." (10-second case for the grinding stone.)
- **Reel 9:** "The salt we use is from [region] — here's why."

#### Format C: "Generation reveal" (huge emotional payoff)
- **Reel 10:** "Recipe written by my grandmother in 1962." (Open old notebook → cut to Amma making it today → cut to a jar shipping out.)
- **Reel 11:** "3 generations, 1 pickle." (Three women — Paati, Amma, daughter — taking turns in the same kadai.)
- **Reel 12:** "My grandfather sold these by bicycle. Today we ship them to Dubai."

#### Format D: "POV / village life"
- **Reel 13:** "POV: It's mango season in our village." (Wake up → walk to tree → pluck → cut → pickle by sundown.)
- **Reel 14:** "A morning at our kitchen, in 60 seconds." (Time-lapse of one full work morning.)
- **Reel 15:** "What 5 kg of mangoes looks like before it becomes 20 jars."

#### Format E: "Customer reaction / unboxing"
- **Reel 16:** A customer in Bangalore opening the package on FaceTime with their mother in Chennai. (Real, raw, asked permission.)
- **Reel 17:** "We sent a jar to Toronto. Watch what happened." (Screen-recorded WhatsApp voice note from a teary NRI customer.)

#### Format F: "Myth-busting / educational"
- **Reel 18:** "3 lies on supermarket pickle labels."
- **Reel 19:** "How to tell a real homemade pickle from a factory one in 5 seconds."
- **Reel 20:** "Why your store-bought pickle smells like vinegar but ours doesn't."

### Reel production rules (non-negotiable for a small team)

1. **Shoot vertical 9:16, in daylight, on a phone — period.** Polished = corporate = unfollow.
2. **First 1.5 seconds is the entire battle.** Open with motion or a question on screen. No slow build.
3. **Trending audio for hooks, original audio for ASMR Reels.** Save 10–15 trending audios from /Reels/Save weekly.
4. **Captions on screen** (people watch on mute). Use Instagram's auto-captions, edit typos.
5. **One Reel = one idea.** Don't pack 5 facts. Reels go viral when they have a single, share-worthy beat.
6. **Length:** 7–15 seconds for ASMR/process; 30–45s for story Reels. Avoid 60s+ unless it's a payoff narrative.
7. **Cover image** designed in Canva so the grid stays on-brand even when Reels dominate it.

**Carousel 1 — "Meet our family" (pinned post forever)**

- Slide 1: "5 things you'll find in supermarket pickles. None of these are in ours."
- Slides 2–6: One ingredient per slide (preservatives, artificial colour, refined oil, citric acid, MSG) with a one-line "we don't use this because…"

**Carousel 4 — "How to eat our [pickle]"**
- 5 ways to use the pickle (with curd rice, dosa, paratha, idli, plain rice) — one photo per slide.

- "Curd rice + Amma's mango pickle in 5 minutes." A real recipe people save.

- "Riya in Berlin ordered 4 jars for her mother's 60th. Here's what happened." (With permission.)

**Carousel 7 — Behind-the-scenes pricing**

- Quote posts: "Pickle is not a side dish. It's a memory." — used sparingly.

## 6. Story Ideas (daily, 4–6 stories/day)

Stories are where the **community** gets built — most won't be seen by non-followers, but they convert lurkers into buyers and followers into super-fans.

### Daily story rhythm

| Time slot | Type | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Morning (8–10 AM) | "Today in our kitchen" | Photo of what's being made today |
| Mid-day (1–3 PM) | Behind-the-scenes / poll | "Mango or Lemon for next batch?" |
| Evening (6–9 PM) | Customer love / order activity | Repost of a tagged story; "12 jars shipped today 🚚" |
| Late evening (occasional) | Personal / human moment | Amma watching TV after a long day, jar-counting on the table |

### High-converting story formats

1. **"Batch is live" countdown sticker.** "60 jars going up tomorrow at 6 PM." Builds scarcity.
2. **Polls** — "What should we pickle next?" "Glass jar or refill pouch?" People who vote feel ownership.
3. **Quiz** — "How long does mango pickle take to ferment?" Educates + boosts story completion.
4. **Question box weekly** — "Ask Amma anything about pickles." Replies become next week's content.
5. **Sliders** — "How spicy do you want it?" 🌶️
6. **Order updates** — "Just shipped to Pune, Coimbatore, Doha 🌍"
7. **Add-yours** — "Show me a jar of pickle from your kitchen." Turns followers into co-creators.
8. **DM CTA** — "Reply 'PICKLE' to get the order link." Trains the algorithm that DMs = engagement.

### Sticker discipline
- 1 interactive sticker (poll/quiz/slider/question) **on at least 3 of every 6 stories.**
- Story completion rate is the single strongest signal IG uses to push your account.

## 7. Hashtags

### Hashtag strategy
- **Use 8–12 per post**, not 30. Anything more and Instagram now de-ranks.
- Mix **3 tiers**: large (>1M posts), mid (100k–1M), small/niche (<100k).
- Place hashtags **in the first comment**, not the caption — keeps captions clean.
- Maintain **3 hashtag sets** rotated by post type so IG doesn't flag you for spammy repetition.

### Set A — Brand & nostalgia (use on family/story posts)
```
#hometaste #ammaskitchen #tasteofhome #homemadewithlove
#desifood #southindianfood #indianhomekitchen #tamilfood
#telugufood #foodmemories #nostalgicfood #appaammaspickles
```

### Set B — Product / category (use on Reels & product posts)
```
#mangopickle #avakaya #lemonpickle #bittergourdpickle
#homemadepickle #indianpickle #achaar #urugai
#picklelovers #picklelife #condiments #foodgift
```

### Set C — Diaspora / discovery (use on customer-story posts)
```
#nri #indiansabroad #indianfoodlovers #desiindubai
#indiansinusa #indianabroad #foodfromhome #mom
#missinghome #indianvegetarian #foodsharing #foodstagram
```

### Branded hashtag (own it, push it)
- **#AmmasJar** — encourage customers to tag every unboxing here. Repost weekly.
- Stencil it on the back of every thank-you card.

### Hashtags to avoid
- Generic mega-tags (#food #foodie #instafood) — you'll never rank, and IG associates you with spam.
- Banned/shadowbanned tags — check periodically with a free tool like Display Purposes.

## 9. Viral Content Opportunities

These are the bets most likely to break out of your follower bubble. Plan for **one viral attempt per week**, accepting that maybe 1 in 8–10 will actually land.

### High-probability viral hooks

1. **"Recipe written by my grandmother in 1962"** — open an actual notebook, page yellowed. Cut to Amma cooking the same recipe today. Cut to a jar going into a courier box. End on the customer opening it. **Why it works:** time-collapse storytelling.

2. **"My grandfather sold these on a bicycle. Today we shipped to Dubai."** — old black-and-white photo → Appa today → courier label with "Dubai" visible. **Why:** generational arc in 15 seconds.

3. **"POV: You're a mango on our tree."** — first-person view from the tree, getting plucked, cut, salted, jarred, shipped. Playful, sharable. **Why:** novel framing.

4. **NRI reaction Reel** — teary voice note from a customer in Toronto/London. Even 1 organic share by them to a diaspora WhatsApp group can spike this.

5. **"What's actually in your supermarket pickle"** — read out 11 ingredients, show ours: 4. Use a trending negative-comparison audio. **Why:** righteous anger = high shares.

6. **"My mother won't share this recipe with me. So I had to start a brand."** — the daughter's POV. Self-deprecating, funny, true. **Why:** relatability with a twist.

7. **"₹350 for a pickle? Here's exactly where every rupee goes."** — radical transparency carousel + Reel. **Why:** trust at scale, often goes viral on LinkedIn too — cross-pollinate.

8. **"The day Amma found out we ship to 12 cities."** — captured reaction. Real surprise, not staged. **Why:** earned emotion.

9. **Festive moment hijack** — when #PongalRecipes or #DiwaliGifts trends, post a Reel within 24 hours of trend pickup. **Why:** ride someone else's reach.

10. **The "stitch / duet" attack on a big food influencer** — when a popular foodie reviews a factory pickle negatively, post a stitched Reel: "Here's what they should be reviewing instead." Tag respectfully. **Why:** rides their audience without being parasitic.

### Viral hygiene
- Watch the first 4 hours after posting. If saves + shares > likes, **boost organically with stories** ("our latest Reel"). Don't pay to boost a winning Reel — IG already is.
- If a Reel crosses 50k views, **immediately pin a comment** with your WhatsApp link. This is where most of the 100 customers will come from.
- Reply to every comment in the first 90 minutes — this is the strongest IG signal.

## 10. Community Building Strategy

The 100 customers is only the surface metric. The real Year-1 asset is a **community that defends, recommends, and reorders**.

### Layer 1 — On Instagram (Top of funnel)

- **Reply to every DM in <2 hours** for the first 90 days. Personally. Use Amma's voice if you can ("Amma will start your batch tomorrow 🙏"). The intimacy is the moat.
- **Comment on followers' posts** — not generically. Find 10 followers/week who post home-cooked food, leave a 1-line genuine comment. They become evangelists.
- **Tag 5–7 followers in your stories** with a poll like "What pickle should we make next?" — they will reshare.
- **Use "Close Friends" as a VIP list** — early customers get added. Send a sneak peek of new SKUs there 48 hours before public.

### Layer 2 — WhatsApp (Mid-funnel — where conversion happens)

- **Every Instagram interaction → WhatsApp.** Bio link, story stickers, comment replies — all roads lead to a single number.
- Set up **WhatsApp Broadcast lists**, segmented:
  - List A: All customers
  - List B: Repeat customers (ordered ≥2 times)
  - List C: NRI customers
  - List D: Friends-of-the-brand (haven't bought yet but engage)
- **Send 1 message/week max** to broadcast lists. Over-messaging kills the goose. Content: new batch alerts, recipe tip, "Amma made too much, 20 jars going first-come."
- **Voice notes from Amma** — for first 100 customers, send a 15-second WhatsApp voice note saying their name + thank you. **Hands-down the highest-LTV ₹0 tactic in this whole plan.**

### Layer 3 — The "100 Founders" club

- The first 100 customers get a **physical "founder" card** (paper, hand-numbered "1 of 100", signed by Amma).
- They get **lifetime 10% off + first dibs on every new batch**.
- They get added to a **private WhatsApp group** ("Amma's Kitchen Insiders") with ~100 members max — Amma occasionally drops recipes, asks for opinions.
- This group will quietly **drive 30–40% of all word-of-mouth** in Year 2.

### Layer 4 — User-generated content engine

- Print a **small card** in every package: *"Tag #AmmasJar — every Sunday Amma picks one to feature."*
- **Repost in stories** within 24 hours — fast reposts train customers that tagging = visibility = dopamine = repeat tagging.
- Run a **monthly UGC mini-contest**: "Best pickle-on-curd-rice photo of the month wins a free 3-pack." Cost: ₹950. Returns: 10–20 user posts that are content + social proof + reach.

### Layer 5 — Influencer partnerships (no money, only product)

- Identify **20 nano-influencers** (5k–30k followers) per month in: South Indian food, Tamil/Telugu/Kannada/Mallu lifestyle, NRI, "missing home" niches.
- Send **personalised DM**, not a template: reference one of their posts.
- Offer: **Free combo of 3 + handwritten note + custom code**. No content demand. About 30% will post organically — and those posts convert better than paid ones.
- Track which 2–3 actually convert. **Re-engage them quarterly with new SKUs.**

### Layer 6 — IRL touchpoints (low frequency, high impact)

- **One society pop-up** per month in your home metro. 4 hours, fold-out table, sample spoons, QR code → WhatsApp. Document it as a Reel — the BTS becomes content.
- **One "kitchen visit" series** — invite a customer (Bangalore-based) to the village to make pickle with Amma for a day. Capture as a 3-Reel mini-doc. **This is your signature piece of content.**

### Layer 7 — Founder-as-character

- **Amma should appear in at least 2 posts/Reels per week.** Not as a model — as herself.
- **Appa is the comic-relief / grumpy charming uncle** — give him a recurring bit ("Appa's pickle reviews", "Appa decides which mangoes are good").
- **The daughter (you)** is the bridge — modern voice translating Amma to the audience.
- Three characters > one brand voice. Always.
